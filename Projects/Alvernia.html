Key elements to preserve and enhance Constraint on every design decision
Three elements are non-negotiable to preserve. All Creative enhancements build on these, not around them.
  1. Alvernia brand presence. The Franciscan Catholic identity carries into every design choice.
  2. Visual consistency across all courses. Learners moving through the MBA see a coherent experience.
  3. Mission Moments callout. The values-connection block already in Matt's template. Preserved as a distinctive feature of Alvernia's approach to online learning.
Critical scope constraint. All proposed enhancements must be easy to implement across (a) the 25 courses Matt has already redesigned, (b) the 10 courses in this SOW, and (c) future course builds. This rules out heavily customized per-course visuals or anything that requires manual rework across 35+ courses. Design decisions should favor modular, template-first patterns that scale without hand-tuning.
Follow-up questions for Matt Sent July 21 with meeting request · awaiting Matt's response
  1. Do you have brand guidelines and assets we can get access to? This is the Brand Foundations "Receive" flow. Anything Alvernia already has (guidelines, colors, typography, logo files) means less work for iDesign Creative to interpret or fill.
  2. Access to campus photography and pencil-drawn style line assets? These are already in Matt's current shell. Confirming access means Creative can reuse rather than recreate, which matters for scope efficiency.
  3. Directions on how to create the pencil-drawn style lines if we cannot access existing assets. If Creative has to fill this, that is Ad-hoc work not sold in the current scope.
  4. What software do you use to create your graphics? Establishes handoff format and whether Creative can pick up Matt's source files or needs to rebuild.
  5. Do you feel like you have what you need to redesign courses? If not, what kinds of graphics do you end up creating from scratch to meet your needs? Surfaces where the gaps are in Alvernia's current process. Also surfaces what "enhancement" actually means to Matt in practical terms, which will shape scope.
  6. Are any of the graphics customized to each course in any way? For example, is the course code and name on the main course banner as part of the graphic itself, or typed in Canvas? This directly affects scope. If course-level identifiers are on the graphic (baked in), that means per-course Course Banner work at typical Templated rates. If they are typed in Canvas as text, that means the banner is Generic (one-time creation) and dramatically lower per-course hours. This one question has major scope implications.
Investigation of the LMS template itself. When Matt provides access to the MBA course and template, Creative needs to see: whether the graphics live inside the LMS template or in Canvas Files or in Pages HTML, what file types are being used (SVG, PNG, JPG), and how they are situated in the LMS template structure. This affects delivery method and future maintenance.

Next steps in order
  1. Get Matt's answers to the follow-up questions. See the Follow-up Questions block below for the six-question list. Answer to question 6 (course code and name baked into graphic vs typed in Canvas) has the most influence on scoping decisions.
  2. Hands-on review of Matt's LMS template and MBA course. Understand where graphics live in the LMS template, what file types are in use, and how they are situated in the LMS structure. Foundation for informed recommendations.
  3. Learning-design and UX research applied to Matt's actual template. Mayer's Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning, Cognitive Load Theory, and visual hierarchy principles. Identify which enhancement candidates would deliver the most meaningful learner impact.
  4. Present research-backed enhancement proposals to Matt. Short list of specific, high-impact enhancement ideas with the reasoning behind each. Not a menu of possibilities. A defensible recommendation.
  5. Finalize scope commitment within available hours. Once enhancement direction is agreed with Matt, lock the scope to fit within the 47.5 hrs of available work (18.25 PP + 29.25 D&D) with per-course D&D design capacity at 1.925 hrs/course.
